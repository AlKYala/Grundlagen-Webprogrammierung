Supply Chain Logistics

Introduction

Supply Chain Logistics is a critical component of modern commerce, encompassing the planning, implementation, and control of efficient, effective forward and reverse flow and storage of goods, services, and related information between the point of origin and the point of consumption to meet customer's requirements. It's a complex network of suppliers, manufacturers, warehouses, distribution centers, and retailers that work together to ensure that products are produced and delivered to the right locations, in the right quantities, and at the right time.

Transportation is a key element in the logistic chain. It involves the movement of products from suppliers to manufacturers, to distribution centers, and ultimately to the end customer. The choice of transportation mode (road, rail, air, or sea) affects the efficiency of supply chain operations, costs, and delivery performance.

Warehousing plays a crucial role in regulating the flow of goods within a supply chain, acting as a buffer to balance demand and supply. Effective warehouse management ensures that goods are stored safely and efficiently, and are readily available for distribution when required. It involves inventory management, picking, packing, and dispatching of goods.
